LOCAL GOVERNMENT- Homeless persons - Local housing authority decision that applicant not having local connection - Whether occupation of interim accommodation pending decision capable of constituting normal residence - Housing Act 1996, ss 198, 199(1), 202

Mohamed v Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council

HL: Lord Lord Slynn of Hadley, Lord Steyn, Lord Hoffmann, Lord Hutton and Lord Hobhouse of Woodborough: 1 November 2001

On a statutory review of a local housing authority's decision to refer an application for accommodation to another authority under s 198 of the Housing Act 1996 on the basis that the applicant had no local connection with itself but had such a connection with that other authority, any occupation by the applicant of interim accommodation within the first authority's district prior to the date of the review could constitute normal residence for the purpose of s 199(1)(a) of the Act and so be taken into account as evidence of a local connection.

The House of Lords so held in dismissing an appeal by Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council against the decision of the Court of Appeal (Henry and Potter LJJ) [2001] QB 97 (1) allowing an appeal by the applicant, Abdulrahman Mohamed, against the decision of the county court judge to confirm a reviewing officer's decision that the applicant had no local connection with Hammersmith; and (2) ordering a fresh review in which the applicant's occupation of interim accommodation within its district was taken into account.

The applicant and his wife had arrived in Hammersmith and sought housing assistance. They were placed in interim accommodation within the borough pending a decision. The authority's decision was that although it had a duty to arrange accommodation for them, it would transfer their application to another authority under s 198 on the ground that they had no local connection with Hammersmith but the wife had a local connection with that other authority. The applicant had sought a review of the decision under s 202 of the Act, but the reviewing officer, having disregarded the period spent in interim accommodation, had affirmed the decision.

LORD SYLNN OF HADLEY said that the authority's principle argument was that the occupation of interim accommodation pending a decision on an application under the Act could not be "normal residence" for the purposes of s 199(1)(a). However, the prima facie meaning of normal residence was a place where at the relevant time the person in fact resided. So long as that place where he ate and slept was voluntarily accepted by him, the reason why he was there rather than somewhere else did not prevent that place from being his normal residence. Interim accommodation could therefore be taken into account in deciding whether a local connection existed. The correct date to decide whether a person had a local connection was not the date of the making of the application but the date of the decision or, if there was a review, the date of the review. As to the material which might be looked at on the review, the reviewing officer could have regard not only to the facts known to the original decision maker but to information relevant to the period before the first decision but only obtained thereafter and to matters occurring after the initial decision.

LORD STEYN, LORD HOFFMANN, LORD HUTTON and LORD HOBHOUSE OF WOODBOROUGH agreed.
