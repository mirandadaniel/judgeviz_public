In re O and N (Minors); In re B (A Minor)

Children — Care proceedings — Threshold conditions — Significant harm suffered by child — Uncertain which parent perpetrator — Future risk — Test to be applied — Children Act 1989, s 31(2)

In re O and N (Minors); In re B (A Minor)

HL: Lord Nicholls of Birkenhead, Lord Hoffmann, Lord Millett, Lord Scott of Foscote and Lord Walker of Gestingthorpe: 3 April 2003
Now published in The Weekly Law Reports: [2003] 2 WLR 1075

Where it was uncertain which of a child's parents had been responsible for causing it significant harm, the judge in proceedings by the local authority under s 31(2) of the Children Act 1989 should proceed on the assumption that each was a possible perpetrator.
The House of Lords so held in allowing an appeal (1) in the first case by Knowsley Metropolitan Borough Council from the decision of the Court of Appeal (Ward LJ and Sir Martin Nourse) sub nom In re O and N (Care: Preliminary Hearing) [2002] 2 FLR 1167 allowing in part the mother's appeal from Judge Downey at Liverpool County Court and!
(2) in the second case dismissing a mother's appeal from the decision of the Court of Appeal (Thorpe, Rix and Arden LJJ) sub nom In re B (Non-Accidental Injury: Compelling Medical Evidence) [2002] 2 FLR 599 allowing an appeal by North Yorkshire County Council and the mother's former partner, KR, from Coleridge J.

LORD NICHOLLS OF BIRKENHEAD said that the appeals related to the "uncertain perpetrator" type of case where a child had suffered physical harm but the court was unable to identify which parent had been the perpetrator or whether both had been. In such cases, the court at the disposal hearing should proceed on the footing that each was a possible perpetrator: see In re H (Minors) (Sexual Abuse: Standard of Proof) [1996] AC 563 and Lancashire County Council v B [2000] 2 AC 147. Assessing future risk on the basis of failure to protect was not a satisfactory alternative.

LORD HOFFMANN, LORD MILLETT, LORD SCOTT OF FOSCOTE and LORD WALKER OF GESTINGTHORPE agreed.
