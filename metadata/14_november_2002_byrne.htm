R v Secretary of State for the Environment, Transport and the Regions, Ex p O'Byrne

Housing — Secure tenancy — Right to buy — Secure tenant of council flat on green belt land — Whether right to buy subject to consent of Secretary of State — Green Belt (London and Home Counties) Act 1938, s 5 — Housing Act 1985, s 118

R v Secretary of State for the Environment, Transport and the Regions, Ex p O'Byrne

HL: Lord Bingham of Cornhill, Lord Hope of Craighead, Lord Hutton, Lord Scott of Foscote and Lord Rodger of Earlsferry: 14 November 2002

The restrictions imposed by s 5 of the Green Belt (London and Home Counties) Act 1938 on the sale by local authorities of green belt land, including the obtaining of the Secretary of State's consent to the sale, applied only to voluntary sales and not to transactions which the local authority was required by statute to make. Therefore s 5 did not operate so as to deprive a secure council tenant of the right, pursuant to s 118 of the Housing Act 1985, to acquire a long lease of her council flat which formed part of a building on green belt land.
The House of Lords so held, dismissing an appeal by the Secretary of State for the Environment, Transport and the Regions from a decision of the Court of Appeal (Thorpe, and Laws LJJ, Buxton LJ dissenting) on 5 April 2001, allowing the appeal of Caroline Martina O'Byrne, from a dismissal by Goldring J on 2 May 2000 of her application for judicial review of the Secretary of State's decision of 3 February 1999 refusing consent for the sale to her by Croydon London Borough Council of a long lease of The Stable Flat, Coombe Wood Park, Croydon ,which she occupied as a secure council tenant.

LORD SCOTT OF FOSCOTE said that the procedural obligations imposed by s 5(1) on the local authority landowner of the land only bit where it was the local authority that wanted, if it could, to carry out the disposition. S 5 was never intended to apply to dispositions of green belt land forced on a local authority by statutory provisions for compulsory acquisition. The transactions contemplated by s 5 were transactions where the local authority was the voluntary disponer and excluded the class of non-voluntary dispositions, compulsory expropriations of which a disposition giving effect to the 1985 right to buy was a member. Therefore s 5 had no application to the 1985 Act right to buy transactions. Hence Miss O'Byrne was entitled to the right to a long lease conferred by s 118 of the 1985 Act and the 1938 Act did not operate to deprive her of that right.
LORD BINGHAM OF CORNHILL and LORD RODGER OF EARLSFERRY delivered concurring speeches.
LORD HOPE OF CRAIGHEAD and LORD HUTTON agreed with Lord Scott.
