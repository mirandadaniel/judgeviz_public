R v Johnstone (Robert)

Crime — Trade mark — Unauthorised use in relation to goods — Infringement — Indication of trade origin — Whether ingredient of offence — Trade Marks Act 1994, ss 11(2)(b), 92

R v Johnstone (Robert)

HL: Lord Nicholls of Birkenhead, Lord Hope of Craighead, Lord Hutton, Lord Rodger of Earlsferry and Lord Walker of Gestingthorpe: 22 May 2003

No offence was committed under s 92 of the Trade Marks Act 1994 where the defendant's use of the mark was not as a trade mark, indicating trade origin.

The House of Lords so held in dismissing an appeal by the Crown from the decision of the Court of Appeal (Criminal Division) (Tuckey LJ, Pumfrey and Burton JJ) [2002] EWCA Crim 194 on 31 January 2002 allowing an appeal by Robert Alexander Johnstone against his conviction at Kingston Crown Court (Judge Mitchell) on 12 counts charging unauthorised use of trade marks in relation to goods, contrary to s 92(1)(c) of the 1994 Act.

LORD NICHOLLS OF BIRKENHEAD said that the case involved bootleg records, in the form of compact discs. The discs had borne the names of the performers, which were registered as trade marks. It had been submitted on behalf of the defendant at trial that to establish an offence under s 92 of the Trade Marks Act 1994 the Crown had to prove a civil infringement of the trade mark in question. The defendant wished to rely on s 11(2)(b) and contend that the use of the performers' names on the discs had not been as an indication of trade origin. It had always been the essence of a trade mark that it was a badge of origin. It was implicit in the provisions of s 92 that the offending use of the sign must be use as a trade mark, an indication of trade origin. That was an ingredient of the offence, to be proved by the prosecution. If the name affixed to the disc and displayed on its packaging was exclusively an indication of the name of the performer and such use was not likely to be understood as indicating any other connection between the performer and the disc, it would be descriptive only and not an indication of trade origin.

LORD HOPE OF CRAIGHEAD, LORD HUTTON and LORD RODGER OF EARLSFERRY agreed with Lord Nicholls of Birkenhead and Lord Walker of Gestingthorpe.

LORD WALKER OF GESTINGTHORPE delivered an opinion concurring in dismissing the appeal.
