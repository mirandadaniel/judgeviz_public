HOUSE OF LORDS — Leave to appeal — Judicial review — Jurisdiction to entertain appeal from dismissal of application — Appellate Jurisdiction Act 1876, s 3 — RSC Ord 59, r 14(3)
Judicial review — Delay — Refusal of relief — "Date when grounds for the application first arose" — Planning permission — Whether date of grant or date of resolution to grant — RSC Ord 53, r 4(1) — CPR r 54.5(1)

R v Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council and another, Ex p Burkett and another

HL: Lord Slynn of Hadley, Lord Steyn, Lord Hope of Craighead, Lord Millett and Lord Phillips of Worth Matravers: 23 May 2002

Grounds for applying for judicial review of a planning permission first arose, under RSC Ord 53, r 4(1), on the grant of permission rather than on the resolution to grant it.
The House of Lords so held in allowing an appeal by Sonia Maria Burkett from the Court of Appeal (Sedley, Ward and Jonathan Parker LJJ) which had on 13 December 2000 dismissed a renewed application by her and her late husband for permission to apply for judicial review of a grant by the local planning authority to the interested party, St George West London Ltd, of an outline planning permission. Richards J had refused their application on the ground of delay.

LORD SLYNN OF HADLEY delivered an opinion agreeing with Lord Steyn in allowing the appeal.
LORD STEYN said that a renewed application to the Court of Appeal under RSC Ord 59, r 14(3) for permission to apply for judicial review was a true appeal: see Kemper Reinsurance Co v Minister of Finance [2000] 1 AC 1. Where the Court of Appeal had granted leave to appeal and heard the appeal, the House of Lords had jurisdiction to grant leave to appeal against the Court of Appeal's refusal of permission. Lord Diplock's extempore observation in In re Poh [1983] 1 WLR 2, 3 was not correct. As to whether, under Ord 53, r 4(1) (now CPR r 54.5(1)), grounds for the application, in respect of a grant of planning permission, first arose at the date of the resolution or at the date of the actual grant of permission, a readily ascertainable starting date was desirable. Legal policy favoured simplicity and certainty, which the judgments of the Court of Appeal and of Laws J in R v Secretary of State for Trade and Industry, Ex p Greenpeace Ltd [1998] EnvLR 415 did not produce. The better interpretation was that time only ran from the grant of permission.
LORD HOPE OF CRAIGHEAD delivered an opinion agreeing with Lord Steyn in allowing the appeal.
LORD MILLETT and LORD PHILLIPS OF WORTH MATRAVERS agreed with Lord Slynn and Lord Steyn.

Appearances: Robert McCracken, Richard Harwood and Angela Ward (Richard Buxton, Cambridge) for Mrs Burkett; Timothy Straker QC and Andrew Tabachnik (Head of Legal Services (Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council) for the local planning authority; Robin Purchas QC and Joanna Clayton (Masons) for the interested party.

Reported by: Michael Gardner, barrister.
